# Information

## Situations

There will be many times when you face uncertain situations. A few of the events that can occur would be:

- ​Mass raids.
- ​Mass spam.
- ​Massive arguments between members.
- ​Much, Much, more!

​In these situations, one major thing you should always remember is to not to panic. Panicking will cause a chain reaction of events that will make the situation much worse.

​​The first thing you should do is mute all users involved in the event.​ Contact a higher up or other moderator if needed.

Keep in mind, situations like these have happened in the past, and the majority of Discord users understand there is no need to panic

## Disclaimers

All staff members are responsible for their actions. We expect you to be able to handle situations appropriately and to be able to use your own judgement.

- You are required to know Discord's Community Guidelines as well as any necessary information within Discord’s Terms of Service, including but not limited to the prohibition of unsolicited advertising within direct messages and the requirement to be at least 13 years old or older to use Discord.
- We use our bot (Haunt) to handle server management, as well as use the bot for moderation commands.
- ​If you’ve got any questions regarding the staff team or other teams, ask an Administrator, and if they are unable to answer your question, you can then ask a Manager or the Owner (developer).

- ​You should not ever refer to the server as “dead” or “dead chat”, as this will reflect negatively on the server.
